Signs You Have a Rodent Infestation

Identifying the signs of a rodent infestation is the first step towards addressing the problem. Ignoring these signs can lead to a larger, more difficult-to-manage situation. Be vigilant and look for these common indicators:

Droppings

Rodent droppings are a clear sign of their presence. Mouse droppings are small (about the size of a grain of rice) and dark, while rat droppings are larger and may be either pointed or blunt at the ends. You'll often find droppings in areas where rodents feed, nest, or travel, such as in pantries, cabinets, attics, basements, and along baseboards. The quantity and freshness of the droppings can also provide clues about the size and activity level of the infestation. Finding fresh droppings is a strong indication of an active problem that needs immediate attention. It's important to wear gloves and a mask when cleaning up droppings, as they can contain harmful bacteria and viruses. Proper sanitation after removal is essential. If you find droppings in food preparation areas, it's crucial to disinfect the surfaces thoroughly. Rodents are known carriers of diseases like Hantavirus and Salmonella, so taking precautions is paramount.
